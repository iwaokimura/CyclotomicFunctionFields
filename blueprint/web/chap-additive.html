1 Additive Polynomials

Throughout this chapter, \(q\) is a prime power and \(\mathbb {F}_q\) denotes the finite field with \(q\) elements. We write \(\mathbb {F}_q[t]= \mathbb {F}_q[t]\) for the polynomial ring and \(\mathbb {F}_q(t)= \mathbb {F}_q(t)\) for the rational function field.

The key players in the theory of cyclotomic function fields are the additive polynomials: polynomials \(P \in \mathbb {F}_q[x]\) satisfying \(P(x+y) = P(x) + P(y)\). Their structure theorem — every additive polynomial is a \(\mathbb {F}_q\)-linear combination of Frobenius powers \(x^{q^i}\) — is the algebraic foundation of the Carlitz module.

References: Hayes  [ Section 1; Goss  [ Chapter 1.

1.1 Definitions

Definition 1 Additive polynomial
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • CyclotomicFunctionFields.IsAdditive

A polynomial \(P \in \mathbb {F}_q[x]\) is additive if

\[ P(x + y) = P(x) + P(y) \]

for all \(x, y\) in every \(\mathbb {F}_q\)-algebra \(K\). More precisely, for every field extension \(K/\mathbb {F}_q\) and all \(x, y \in K\),

\[ \mathrm{aeval}(x, P) + \mathrm{aeval}(y, P) = \mathrm{aeval}(x+y, P). \]

✓

For \(n \geq 0\), the \(n\)-th Frobenius power polynomial is

\[ \tau ^n := x^{q^n} \; \in \; \mathbb {F}_q[x]. \]

In Lean this is frobeniusPower n, defined as Polynomial.monomial (q^n) 1.

1.2 Basic closure properties

Proposition 3 Sum of additive polynomials is additive
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 1

Lean declarations

  • CyclotomicFunctionFields.IsAdditive.add

If \(P\) and \(Q\) are additive, then so is \(P + Q\).

Proof ▶

Direct computation:

\begin{align*} (P+Q)(x+y) & = P(x+y) + Q(x+y) \\ & = (P(x)+P(y)) + (Q(x)+Q(y)) \\ & = (P(x)+Q(x)) + (P(y)+Q(y)) \\ & = (P+Q)(x) + (P+Q)(y). \qedhere \end{align*}
Proposition 4 Scalar multiple of an additive polynomial is additive
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 1

Lean declarations

  • CyclotomicFunctionFields.IsAdditive.smul

If \(P\) is additive and \(a \in \mathbb {F}_q\), then \(a \cdot P\) is additive.

Proof ▶

\((aP)(x+y) = a \cdot P(x+y) = a(P(x)+P(y)) = (aP)(x) + (aP)(y)\).

Proposition 5 Composition of additive polynomials is additive
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 1

Lean declarations

  • CyclotomicFunctionFields.IsAdditive.comp

If \(P\) and \(Q\) are additive, then so is \(P \circ Q\).

Proof ▶

\((P \circ Q)(x + y) = P(Q(x+y)) = P(Q(x)+Q(y)) = P(Q(x)) + P(Q(y))\), using additivity of \(Q\) then \(P\).

1.3 Frobenius powers are additive

Proposition 6 Frobenius powers are additive
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 1
  • Definition 2

Lean declarations

  • CyclotomicFunctionFields.IsAdditive.frobeniusPower_isAdditive

For every \(n \geq 0\), the polynomial \(x^{q^n}\) is additive.

Proof ▶

In a field \(K\) of characteristic \(p\) with \(q = p^r\), the Frobenius map \(x \mapsto x^q\) is a ring homomorphism, so it is additive. Iterating, \(x \mapsto x^{q^n}\) is also additive:

\[ (x + y)^{q^n} = x^{q^n} + y^{q^n}. \]

In Lean this follows from add_pow_char_pow.

1.4 Structure of additive polynomials

We now work towards the main structure theorem. The key step is showing that the coefficient \([x^n]P\) vanishes whenever \(n\) is not a power of \(q\).

Lemma 7 Constant term of an additive polynomial is zero
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 1

Lean declarations

  • CyclotomicFunctionFields.IsAdditive.constant_term_zero

If \(P\) is additive, then \(P(0) = 0\), i.e. the constant coefficient of \(P\) is \(0\).

Proof ▶

Setting \(x = y = 0\) in \(P(x+y) = P(x)+P(y)\) gives \(P(0) = 2P(0)\), hence \(P(0) = 0\). In Lean, evaluate over \(\mathbb {F}_q\) itself and simplify.

Lemma 8 Non-\(q\)-power coefficients vanish
#

Uses

  • Definition 1
  • Lemma 7

Lean declarations

  • CyclotomicFunctionFields.IsAdditive.coeff_zero_of_not_q_power

Let \(P\) be additive and let \(n \geq 1\) be a positive integer that is not a power of \(q\). Then \([x^n]P = 0\).

Proof ▶

Since \(n\) is not a power of \(q = p^r\), and in particular not a power of the characteristic \(p\), by Lucas’s theorem there exists an integer \(k\) with \(0 {\lt} k {\lt} n\) such that \(\binom {n}{k} \not\equiv 0 \pmod{p}\).

Working over the infinite field \(K = \mathbb {F}_q(t)\) (which has characteristic \(q\)), the functional equation \(P(X+Y) = P(X)+P(Y)\) holds for all \(X, Y \in K\). Lifting this to an identity of polynomials in \(K[X,Y]\) (valid because \(K\) is infinite) and comparing coefficients of \(X^k Y^{n-k}\):

  • the right-hand side contributes \(0\) (no mixed term in \(P(X)+P(Y)\) since \(0 {\lt} k {\lt} n\));

  • the left-hand side contributes \([x^n]P \cdot \binom {n}{k}\) via the binomial theorem.

Hence \([x^n]P \cdot \binom {n}{k} = 0\) in \(K\). Since \(\binom {n}{k} \neq 0\) in \(K\) and \(K\) is a field, \([x^n]P = 0\).

Remark 9
#

The step “lift pointwise identity to polynomial identity” uses MvPolynomial.funext (polynomial equality from pointwise equality over an infinite ring). This step is the remaining sorry in the Lean proof.

Lemma 10 Support contained in \(q\)-power exponents
✓
#

Uses

  • Lemma 8

Lean declarations

  • CyclotomicFunctionFields.IsAdditive.support_subset_q_powers

If \(P\) is additive, then every \(n \in \operatorname{supp}(P)\) is a power of \(q\).

Proof ▶

Immediate from Lemma 8: if \(n \in \operatorname{supp}(P)\) then \([x^n]P \neq 0\), so \(n\) must be a power of \(q\).

Theorem 11 Structure theorem for additive polynomials
#

Uses

  • Lemma 10
  • Definition 2

Lean declarations

  • CyclotomicFunctionFields.IsAdditive.structure_theorem

Every additive polynomial \(P \in \mathbb {F}_q[x]\) can be written uniquely as

\[ P = \sum _{i=0}^{N} a_i \, x^{q^i} \]

for some \(N \geq 0\) and coefficients \(a_i \in \mathbb {F}_q\). In other words, \(P\) is an \(\mathbb {F}_q\)-linear combination of Frobenius power polynomials.

Proof ▶

By Lemma 10, the support of \(P\) is contained in \(\{ q^0, q^1, q^2, \ldots \} \). Writing \(P = \sum _{n \in \operatorname{supp}(P)} [x^n]P \cdot x^n\) and reindexing over \(q\)-power exponents yields the desired form. (Uniqueness is clear from the linear independence of monomials.)
